some comments

The functions here are cobb douglas, so a change in px causes:

  • movement along the x(px)
  • movement along h(px)
  • no change in y(py)
  • shifting of y_c(py)

In this case, the income and sub effects for good y in response to a change in px are equal and opposite.

x_c^*=u_0 \big ( \dfrac{p_y}{p_x} \cdot \dfrac{\alpha}{1-\alpha} \big ) ^ {1-\alpha}

the initial marshallian and hicksian points are the same. but their response allocations are different.

therefore, while there are 4 points, 2 of them overlap, so we've got effectively 3 unique points
